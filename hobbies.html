    • ▶ ThinkPad Modding & Optimization

    It may sound strange to the uninitiated, but ThinkPads are seriously neat laptops. I've owned five and kept three of those, being a T520, X220, and T60.

    T520

  • OS: dual-boot Linux Mint and Windows 10
  • Screen: upgraded from 1366x768p to 1920x1080p
  • CPU: upgraded from dual-core i5-2520M to quad-core i7-2760QM
  • RAM: upgraded from 4GB single-channel @ 1333MHz to 8GB dual-channel @ 1600MHz
  • WiFi: upgraded from stock to Dell Intel AC 7260 (300 Mbps to 1200 Mbps)
  • BIOS: modded to unlock advanced features and remove whitelist

    • The first issue I ran into was poor CPU thermals both at idle and under load. After all, this model's fan wasn't intended to cool a quad-core i7. To bring down the heat, I slightly lowered the max CPU frequency and adjusted the fan speeds and trip points in the modified BIOS. By defualt, these were set to 55C and 71C with speeds of ~1200RPM and ~2400RPM. By changing the lo trip point to 47C and the hi trip point to 63C with respective speeds of 2400 RPM and 6400 RPM, I was able to prevent thermal throttling from ever being in the cards. And it may seem counter-productive, but enabling hyper-threading was also beneficial. With hyper-threading enabled, CPU core temps were slightly higher under load (2-3 C). But tasks finished so much faster that the CPU was able to cool sooner and had a much lower average temperature over time.

    X220 & T60

  • OS: Debian and Zorin OS, respectively
  • Secondary storage: HDD to SSD

    • No other modifications were made to these laptops. But eventually I intend on replacing the X220's stock BIOS with coreboot and upgrading the screen to an IPS. The T60 was the last model without Intel ME, so I keep it around for privacy reasons.
